Common Questions

Is telehealth safe and effective?

Yes. For most primary care needs—acute illnesses, chronic disease management, medication refills, and wellness visits—telehealth is as effective as in-person care. Studies back this up. If I determine you need hands-on assessment, labs, or imaging, I'll tell you—and I offer house calls for those situations.

Do you take insurance?

No. I don't bill insurance, which means no co-pays, no claims, and no surprise bills. You pay me directly. You can still use your insurance for labs, imaging, specialists, and prescriptions—I'll give you orders and referrals just like any primary care provider.

What if I need labs or imaging?

I can order labs, X-rays, ultrasounds, and any other tests you need. You'll use your insurance (or pay cash) at a local lab or imaging center. I'll review your results, explain what they mean, and create a treatment plan with you.

Can you prescribe medications?

Yes. As a Family Nurse Practitioner, I can prescribe medications, including controlled substances (with appropriate evaluation). I send prescriptions electronically to your preferred pharmacy.

How do house calls work?

You schedule a house call in advance (typically 1-3 days out). I bring everything I need for a comprehensive visit: stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, basic point-of-care testing supplies. Visits last 30-45 minutes. Members receive discounted rates.

What's your cancellation policy?

Please give 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Life happens—I'm flexible—but last-minute cancellations may incur a fee for house calls.
